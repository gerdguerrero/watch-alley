MoonSwatch resale cools — time to buy.

By The Watch Alley · 5 min read

Three years after the launch hysteria, the Bioceramic Omega × Swatch lineup has finally settled. Secondary prices on the Mission to Mars and the Mission to the Moon are trading at or below retail in Manila, and the longer flips — Mission to Mercury, Mission to Pluto — are moving at a small premium that no longer screams scarcity tax.

That changes the math. For the past two years a MoonSwatch was a flip play first and a watch second. From Q1 2026 onward it is a watch you can buy because you actually want to wear it.

Why prices cooled

  • Swatch boutiques are now reliably stocked in Manila and Singapore. Two-hour queues at the BGC store became fifteen-minute walk-ins through 2025.
  • The shock-collab novelty is gone. Buyers who were going to flip have flipped.
  • Real wearers settled in. The pieces that actually got worn for two years now show light scuffs that re-set the secondary baseline.
“The MoonSwatch is finally a watch you can buy because you want to wear it. The flip premium is gone.”

What to look for if you are buying pre-owned

  • Strap condition. The original Velcro stretches with wear. Replacements are inexpensive but a tired original is a fair negotiation point.
  • Crown function. The Bioceramic case is more flexible than steel; an over-pulled crown can develop play. Ask for a video of the chrono pushers and crown going through the positions.
  • Battery date. The 1.6-volt is a 24-month service item. If the seller can’t recall when it was last changed, factor a battery into the buy price.
  • Box and outer pouch. The black-matte Bioceramic box is part of the gift-economy of these pieces. Missing box can knock 5 to 8 percent off resale later.

Our position

We will keep carrying complete-set MoonSwatches as we find them, priced to move rather than priced to sit. If you have wanted one and waited, the next 90 days are the cleanest window the secondary market has offered since launch.
