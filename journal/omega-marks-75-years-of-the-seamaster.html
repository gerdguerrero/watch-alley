Omega marks 75 years of the Seamaster.

By The Watch Alley · 3 min read

Omega closed Watches & Wonders 2025 with a quiet but consequential lineup: commemorative editions across the entire professional Seamaster range, plus a renewed Aqua Terra reference catalogue. No big-stage fireworks. The story is in the breadth — the Seamaster is being positioned for another decade as the brand’s working line.

For the Philippine pre-owned market the news matters more than the spec sheets. Anniversary years tend to lift secondary prices on the prior generation by 8 to 12 percent over six months, and the lift sticks longest on the references that did not get a 75th-anniversary refresh. Those become the value plays.

Where the value sits

The Aqua Terra 41 mm in the 220.10 references is the obvious one. It was the daily-wear sleeper of the line for years; it is now the cleanest entry into the modern Seamaster identity at a price that has quietly stopped falling. The Seamaster 300m in the original ceramic-bezel 210 references is the same play in a sportier register.

“Anniversary years lift the prior generation by 8 to 12 percent. The lift sticks longest on references that did not get a refresh.”

What we tell collectors

If you have wanted a modern Seamaster, the next two quarters are the right window. Buy on condition, on full set, and on a verified service record if the piece is older than four years. Skip anything with a refinished case unless the seller is upfront about it and the price reflects the work — refinishing softens the lugs in ways photos can’t hide.
